# Brand Voice — Quickstop Super Deli

## Personality

Think: the guy behind the counter who knows your regular order. Friendly, fast, no-nonsense. Not trying to be fancy — just good food, quick service, and a familiar face.

## Tone

- **Friendly and warm** — like talking to a neighbor
- **Casual** — not stiff, not corporate, not trying too hard
- **Confident** — we know our food is good, no need to oversell
- **Occasionally funny** — a little humor when it fits, never forced
- **Real** — honest, genuine, no fake enthusiasm

## Language

Use NYC / Long Island deli language naturally:
- "Hero" not "sub" or "submarine sandwich"
- "BEC" for bacon egg and cheese
- "On line" not "in line"
- "Regular coffee" means milk and sugar
- "The usual" — regulars know what this means
- Keep sentences short. Write like you talk.

## What We Sound Like

**Good:**
- "5 AM. Coffee's ready. BEC is rolling. See you before the train."
- "Cold out there? We got hot coffee and a chicken cutlet hero with your name on it."
- "Forgot milk on the way home? We got you. Open til 7."

**Bad:**
- "Start your morning right with our artisanal breakfast offerings!"
- "We're thrilled to announce our new seasonal menu items!"
- "Don't forget to check out our amazing deals this week!"

## Visual Style

- **Colors:** Bold reds, warm yellows, cream backgrounds (Deli Red #D62828, Deli Yellow #F7B32B, Deli Cream #FFF8E7)
- **Photos:** Real food on the counter, in wax paper, on deli paper. Not styled or staged. Phone camera is fine — just good lighting.
- **Vibe:** Classic deli signage meets modern social media. Think hand-written specials board, not Canva template.

## What to Avoid

- Corporate marketing speak ("leverage," "optimize," "curate," "elevate")
- Influencer tone ("obsessed," "literally dying," "you guys")
- Generic food captions ("Yum!" "So delicious!" "Food goals!")
- Overly polished or stock-photo aesthetics
- Emojis overload — one or two is fine, not a string of 10
