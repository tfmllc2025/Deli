# Review Strategy — Getting More Google Reviews

Google reviews are the #1 way to get more customers for a local deli. A deli with 4.5+ stars and 100+ reviews will show up at the top of Google search results. This is the highest priority.

## Goal

- Get 2-3 new Google reviews per week
- Maintain a 4.5+ star average
- Reply to every single review (good and bad) within 1-2 days

## How to Ask for Reviews

### The Best Way: Ask in Person

When a regular comes in and says something nice — "great sandwich," "love the coffee," etc. — that's the moment. Say something like:

> "Hey thanks, I appreciate that. If you get a chance, would you leave us a Google review? It really helps."

Don't ask every person every time. Ask the happy ones, especially regulars who you know by name.

### Counter Sign / QR Code

Put a small sign near the register:

```
LOVE THE FOOD?
Leave us a Google review!
[QR CODE]
It takes 30 seconds and helps us a lot.
```

**How to make the QR code:**
1. Go to Google and search for your deli
2. Click on your listing
3. Click "Write a review" — copy that URL from your browser
4. Go to qr-code-generator.com (free)
5. Paste the URL
6. Download the QR code image
7. Print it and put it by the register

### On Receipts (If Your Register Supports It)

Add a line at the bottom of receipts:
```
Thanks! Leave us a Google review — it means a lot.
```

## How to Reply to Reviews

See the templates in `content/templates/review-response.md` for ready-to-paste responses.

**Key rules:**
- Reply to EVERY review — good and bad
- Good reviews: keep it short and personal
- Bad reviews: stay calm, say sorry, offer the phone number
- Reply within 1-2 days

## What to Track

Check these once a week:
- How many new Google reviews this week?
- What's the current star rating?
- Any bad reviews that need a response?

Also check (once a month):
- DoorDash rating
- GrubHub rating
- Yelp rating (if listed)
